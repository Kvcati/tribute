Biography

Sojourner Truth, born Isabella Baumfree, was an African American abolitionist, women's rights activist, and author who escaped slavery and became a powerful voice for equality, famously known for her "Ain't I a Woman?" speech.

Early Life and Slavery: Born into slavery in Ulster County, New York, around 1797, she experienced the brutality of the system, including being sold multiple times and witnessing the separation of her family.

Escape to Freedom: In 1826, she escaped with her infant daughter to freedom.
